Agrani Bank PLC ‚Äì Internal Practice Summary

‚úÖ When waiver/reduction is usually considered +
  • To recover principal from long overdue / classified loans
  • To settle cases where borrower is paying but cannot bear full penal interest
  • To close legal cases or reduce litigation cost
  • For realistic adjustment of loan liability for negotiation or compromise settlement
üìå Common internal conditions used in waiver proposals +
  • Borrower must pay minimum principal portion before waiver approval
  • Waiver is normally applied only for classified period interest (not always for regular period)
  • Waiver is linked with settlement schedule and borrower ability
  • Approval authority depends on amount and classification category
üìÑ Waiver proposal structure (typical format) +
  • Loan liability breakdown (principal + interest + charges)
  • Recovery position (deposit, cash, collateral, security value)
  • Cost of Fund (COF) calculation and justification
  • Borrower commitment letter / settlement agreement
  • Credit risk notes + legal status

Bangladesh Bank (BB) ‚Äì Regulatory Framework Summary

‚úÖ BB focus for waiver/reduction approval logic +
  • Waiver must not encourage willful default culture
  • Recovery priority must remain principal-first
  • Bank must ensure loan risk classification remains correct
  • Waiver should improve actual recovery & cash flow, not just book adjustment
üìå Key concepts banks must consider +
  • Loan rescheduling vs waiver: rescheduling rearranges payment, waiver reduces payable amount.
  • Any waiver must be supported by proper board approval / delegated authority.
  • Interest waiver is normally linked to settlement plan & recovery record.
  • Waiver should consider provisioning policy and IFRS-related impact on bank statements.
üßæ Documentation expected in BB compliant waiver cases +
  • Borrower financial analysis and repayment capability
  • Legal case status, suit value, estimated recovery and collateral evaluation
  • Internal audit and compliance note (where required)
  • Proper approval note, committee minute and sanction reference

Best Practice Checklist (for Loan Officers)

‚úÖ Before recommending waiver +
  • Confirm loan classification date and expiry date carefully
  • Confirm debit interest entries and total uncharged interest period
  • Ensure borrower has valid source of repayment / cash recovery plan
  • Calculate COF properly to avoid approval mistake

  • Wrong CSV statement (missing rows/columns)
