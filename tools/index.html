Understanding EMI Calculations

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount paid by a borrower every month until a loan is fully repaid. It is determined by three primary factors:

  • Loan amount (Principal)
  • Interest rate
  • Loan tenure (number of months or years)

Our EMI calculators demonstrate both the Declining Balance method and Equal Principal method so users can clearly see how interest is charged over time. Many borrowers do not realize that the interest portion is higher in the early months and decreases gradually as the principal reduces. Understanding this structure helps make better financial decisions.

Loan Eligibility Estimation

Before approving a loan, banks evaluate repayment capacity based on income, obligations, and internal policy rules. Our eligibility tools provide a simplified estimation based on common banking ratios (such as 80% or 2/3 income rules).

These estimations are intended to help users understand approximate affordability. Final approval decisions depend on official bank policy, documentation, credit history, and regulatory guidelines.
