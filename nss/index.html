‚úÖ How to Use This NSS Form Tool
  1. Select the NSS scheme type (Family / 5-Year / 3-Month / Pensioner).
  2. Fill buyer details, nominee details and bank account information carefully.
  3. Click Generate PDF to download your form.
  4. Print the PDF, sign it, attach required documents and submit to bank.

Tip: Always verify your information before printing to avoid rejection at the bank.

üìÑ Required Documents (Usually Asked by Banks)
  • NID (Buyer + Nominee)
  • Buyer photo + nominee photo
  • Bank account details (Account number + routing)
  • Nominee relationship proof (sometimes)
  • Pensioner certificate (for Pensioner Sanchayapatra)

Note: Document requirements may vary slightly by bank branch.

üí∞ Profit Rate + Tax Deduction (Source: Govt Site)

Profit rate varies by scheme and investment amount. Government can update rates anytime. Always verify current profit rates from official website or your bank.

Tax: Usually 5% source tax applies up to total investment of 5,00,000 BDT (for certain schemes), and 10% above that. Pensioner Sanchayapatra often gets tax exemption up to 5,00,000 BDT.

üè¶ Where You Can Buy / Encash NSS
  • National Savings Bureau Offices
  • Bangladesh Bank branches
  • Scheduled commercial banks
  • Post offices (for selected schemes)

You can buy and encash NSS from these channels depending on scheme availability.

‚ùì FAQ ‚Äì Common Questions

Q1: Can I submit this form digitally?
Usually no. Most banks require printed signed copy.

Q2: Can I buy NSS jointly?
Some schemes allow joint purchase. Family Sanchayapatra cannot be purchased jointly.

Q3: What happens if I encash before maturity?
Early encashment gives profit based on government rules and may reduce profit.

Q4: Will this tool store my data?
No. All information stays in your browser. Only PDF is generated locally.
