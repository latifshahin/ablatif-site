Understanding DSCR: How Banks Measure Loan Repayment Ability

When banks evaluate a loan proposal, one of the most important ratios they consider is DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio). It helps answer a simple question: Can the borrower comfortably repay the loan from cash flow?

What Is DSCR?

DSCR measures the relationship between a borrowerâ€™s net operating income and their total loan repayment obligation.

Basic formula: 

	DSCR = Net Operating Income / Total Debt Obligations

A DSCR above 1.00 means the borrower generates enough cash to cover loan payments. A DSCR below 1.00 indicates repayment stress.

  • DSCR 1.10 - 1.24 - Acceptable but needs monitoring
  • DSCR < 1.10 - High risk, often leads to rejection

Exact requirements vary by bank, loan type, and industry risk.

What Is Included in Net Operating Income?

  • Operating profit
  • Non-cash expenses added back (e.g., depreciation)
  • Less: taxes and owner withdrawals (where applicable)

What Counts as Debt Obligations?

  • Existing term loan installments
  • Proposed loan installment
  • Lease payments
  • Other fixed financial commitments

Common DSCR Mistakes Borrowers Make

  • Ignoring existing personal loans
  • Overestimating future income
  • Underestimating working capital needs
  • Assuming collateral can replace cash flow

How to Improve DSCR

  • Reduce unnecessary debt
  • Increase equity contribution
  • Improve operating efficiency
  • Restructure loan tenure to reduce installments

Banks prefer realistic projections supported by historical performance. A strong DSCR builds confidence and increases approval chances.
